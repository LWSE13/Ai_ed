Overview

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is increasingly used in education to support teaching and learning through tools such as adaptive learning systems, intelligent tutoring, and automated assessment and feedback. These systems can analyse learner data to tailor content and support, and are becoming a notable part of modern educational practice (Wang et al., 2024; Garzón et al., 2025).

Rather than replacing teachers, AI is typically framed as a support technology that can reduce routine workload and provide additional insight into student progress. This can help educators spend more time on high-value tasks such as guidance, classroom interaction, and personalised support (Merikko and Kivimäki, 2022; Garzón et al., 2025).

At its core, AI in education focuses on using data-driven methods to detect patterns in learning, provide timely feedback, and support decision-making for teaching and student support. When implemented responsibly, these tools are intended to complement human teaching, not substitute it, helping teachers prioritise meaningful interaction and pedagogical judgement (Wang et al., 2024; Merikko and Kivimäki, 2022).

Personalized Learning

AI enables learning systems to adapt content, pace, and feedback to individual students. By analysing learner data, AI can identify strengths and gaps in understanding, allowing instruction to be personalised rather than standardised (Vorobyeva et al., 2025; Garzón et al., 2025; Wang et al., 2024).

Time Savings for Teachers

AI can automate routine tasks such as grading, feedback and administrative reporting, reducing workload and enabling teachers to focus on planning and student support (Garzón et al., 2025; Merikko and Kivimäki, 2022).

Accessibility & Inclusion

AI can support inclusive learning through assistive and adaptive tools, such as speech recognition, translation and personalised scaffolding for diverse learners (Garzón et al., 2025; Labadze et al., 2023).

Data-Driven Insights

AI can analyse educational data to identify patterns in engagement and performance, supporting early intervention and evidence-based decisions (Wang et al., 2024; Garzón et al., 2025).

24/7 Support

AI chatbots and tutoring systems can provide on-demand help outside class time by answering questions and offering explanations, supporting self-paced learning (Labadze et al., 2023; Vorobyeva et al., 2025).
