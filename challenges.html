Challenges
and Ethics

As artificial intelligence becomes more integrated into classrooms, it brings both exciting opportunities and complex ethical challenges. From protecting student data to ensuring fairness in algorithms, educators must carefully consider how AI is implemented and used. This page explores key issues surrounding AI in education

It is important to develop responsible, human-centred technology that supports learning while upholding trust and integrity. Below, we've highlighted key ethical challenges and concerns related to privacy, bias, transparency, and accountability. Click on the accordion sections below to learn more about each topic.

AI systems in education often collect vast amounts of student data, including learning patterns, behaviors, and personal information. Protecting this sensitive data is crucial to maintain student privacy and comply with regulations like FERPA and GDPR. Schools must ensure that AI tools have robust security measures and transparent data policies.

AI algorithms can perpetuate or amplify existing biases if trained on biased data sets. In education, this could lead to unfair assessment outcomes, unequal learning opportunities, or discriminatory recommendations. It's essential to regularly audit AI systems for bias and ensure diverse, representative training data to promote equity in education.

Many AI systems operate as "black boxes," making decisions without clear explanations of how they arrived at those conclusions. In educational settings, transparency is vital so teachers and students understand how AI tools make recommendations or assessments. Clear documentation and explainable AI help build trust and enable informed decision-making.

The use of AI tools raises questions about academic integrity and authentic learning. While AI can assist with learning, there's concern about students using AI to complete assignments without genuine understanding. Educators must establish clear guidelines on appropriate AI use and design assessments that encourage critical thinking and original work rather than AI-generated responses.
